\section linearnrpsiderophores Linear Nonribosomal Peptide Siderophores

<p>There are no special requirements for identification of desferri-forms of linear siderophores which are nonribosomal peptides (see [Linear Peptides](@ref lineardetail)).
However, the [Precursor Ion Adduct](@ref settings_adduct) must be set up for identification of ferri-forms of siderophores.
The peaks of isotopes having natural abundances greater than 3% are generated automatically in theoretical peaklists next to the peaks of most abundant isotopes.
If a metal is included in the precursor ion adduct field, the generated theoretical fragments are suffixed with its name and corresponding theoretical isotope fragments are generated 
(e.g., the fragments B4_Fe and B4_54Fe are generated instead of B4 when precursor ion adduct "FeH-2" is used).
</p>

\section linearpolyketidenomenclature Nomenclature of Linear Polyketide Series

<p>
A polyketide is composed of a sequence of ketide building blocks which eliminate hydrogen atoms from both termini or eliminate a molecule of water.
A linear polyketide can have N-terminus and C-terminus; but also two N-termini or two C-termini. 
CycloBranch generates 8 basic series of fragment ions for linear polyketides (L and R stand for left and right, respectively):
<ul>
<li> <sup>L</sup>b (left b-ion)
<li> <sup>L</sup>b + 2H (left b-ion + 2H)
<li> <sup>R</sup>b (right b-ion)
<li> <sup>R</sup>b + 2H (right b-ion + 2H)
<li> <sup>L</sup>y (left y-ion)
<li> <sup>L</sup>y - 2H (left y-ion - 2H)
<li> <sup>R</sup>y (right y-ion)
<li> <sup>R</sup>y - 2H (right y-ion - 2H)
</ul> 
</p>

<p>
If a metal is included in the precursor ion adduct field, the generated theoretical fragments are suffixed with its name and corresponding theoretical isotope fragments are generated 
(e.g., the fragments LB3+2H_Fe and LB3+2H_54Fe are generated instead of LB3+2H when precursor ion adduct "FeH-2" is used). See also [Precursor Ion Adduct](@ref settings_adduct). 
A mass spectrometry nomenclature for linear polyketide siderophores is shown in the following figure.
</p>

\section linearpolyketidecandidatedetection Linear Polyketide Sequence Detection

<p>
The detection of linear polyketide sequence candidates is similar to the detection of linear peptide sequence candidates. See [Linear Sequence Detection](@ref linearcandidatedetection).
</p>

<p>
A difference is that four starting points are applicable to linear polyketides: H<sup>+</sup>, H<sup>+</sup> + 2H, H<sub>3</sub>O<sup>+</sup> and H<sub>3</sub>O<sup>+</sup> - 2H.
If a ferri-form of a siderophore is being detected, masses of all starting points must be increased by mass of Fe - 3H. The process is analogous for other metals.
</p>
