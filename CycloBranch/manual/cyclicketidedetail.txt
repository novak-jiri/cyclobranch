\section cyclicpolyketidenomenclature Nomenclature of Cyclic Polyketide Series

<p>The nomenclature of cyclic polyketides is based on the nomenclatures of linear polyketides and cyclic peptides. 
See [Nomenclature of Linear Polyketide Series](@ref linearpolyketidenomenclature) and [Nomenclature of Cyclic Series](@ref cyclicnomenclature).
CycloBranch generates 2 basic series of fragment ions for cyclic polyketides (L and R stand for left and right, respectively):
<ul>
<li> <sup>L</sup>b (left b-ion)
<li> <sup>L</sup>b + 2H (left b-ion + 2H)
</ul>
</p>

<p>
<sup>R</sup>b and <sup>R</sup>b + 2H series series are not generated because the fragment ions are redundant with the series <sup>L</sup>b and <sup>L</sup>b + 2H.
The fragment ion series <sup>L</sup>y, <sup>L</sup>y - 2H, <sup>R</sup>y and <sup>R</sup>y - 2H (utilized in [Nomenclature of Linear Polyketide Series](@ref linearpolyketidenomenclature)) are 
not applicable to cyclic polyketide siderophores due to head-to-tail cyclization.
</p>

<p>
Like in the case of cyclic peptides, CycloBranch generates theoretical series of ions <sup>i-j,L</sup>b and <sup>i-j,L</sup>b + 2H where i-j are numbers of 
building blocks between which a ring having n building blocks is opened (i,j <= n; i=1, j=n; or i=j+1, otherwise). 
CycloBranch also generates reversed series of ions <sup>j-i,L</sup>b and <sup>j-i,L</sup>b + 2H. 
</p>

<p>
When a metal is included in the precursor ion adduct field, the generated theoretical fragments are suffixed with its name and corresponding theoretical isotope fragments are generated 
(e.g., the fragments LB3+2H_Fe and LB3+2H_54Fe are generated instead of LB3+2H when precursor ion adduct "FeH-2" is used). See also [Precursor Ion Adduct](@ref settings_adduct). 
A mass spectrometry nomenclature for cyclic polyketide siderophore desferrioxamine E is shown in the following figure.
</p>

</p>

\section cyclicpolyketidecandidatedetection Cyclic Polyketide Sequence Detection

<p>
The detection of cyclic polyketide sequence candidates is based on [Linear Polyketide Sequence Detection](@ref linearpolyketidecandidatedetection) and 
[Cyclic Sequence Detection](@ref cycliccandidatedetection). The starting points applicable to cyclic polyketides are H<sup>+</sup> and H<sup>+</sup> + 2H.
</p>
